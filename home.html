Who am I?

My name is Julian Krese and I'm currently a third-year Computer Science Major and Data Science Minor at the Univeristy of Virginia. Welcome to my site! With applications in full swing, I realized my resume only truly displayed so much of who I am and what defines me. It does a good job of showing my skills, but doesn't show me. That brings us to this website- I realized it would be a great way to show some very basic web-development skills while serving as a introduction for anyone who visits it. The website itself is made of the classic HTML, CSS, and JavaScript trio, with a little Bootstrap throw in there for some great effects such as the slide carousel above.

So who is Julian Krese besides a random Computer Science student?

Well, if you didn't get the memo above, I love the outdoors. Although I love hiking and travel and just exploring in general, fly fishing takes the cake in terms of my passions. Ever since my dad taught my brother and me as kids, we've both become advid anglers. It's taken me all over the place; from Maine to Montana to the Bahamas. Beyond fly fishing, I love to golf and play soccer. I played soccer in high school but now just stick to intramurals. As for golf, I've slowly been wittling down my handicap and shoot in the mid-to-low 80's, so I can hold my own but there's still lots to work on.

So how does a outdoorsmen find his way into a career behind a screen?

Growing up, I always loved building things. Whether just Lego Sets, duct tape wallets, or cubes out of grocery store twisty-ties, I was always building. My magnum-opus was a ping pong ball dispenser made out of an old cereal box. This love for building was always there, but as I went through school I started to lose touch with it more as it was less present in my life. Luckily, I decided to take in a Computer Science course in highschool, which allowed me to jump back and reconnect with my inner child, switching from the physical world to the digital world. Because of this class, I enrolled in a Intro Computer SCience course, where I learned the basics of programming and got to build a game through PyGame. This class helped me confirm Computer Science was the route I wanted to go down. Now, I'm trying to turn my love of coding into a professional career.
